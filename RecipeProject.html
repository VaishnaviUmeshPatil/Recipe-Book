Gulab Jamun

Ingredients

  • Gulabjamun mix
  • Milk
  • Oil
  • Sugar

Recipe

  1. Make sugar syrup by boiling sugar, water, cardamom, and rose water for 5–7 minutes and keep it warm.
  2. Prepare dough by mixing milk powder, maida, baking soda, ghee, and enough milk to form a soft dough.
  3. Shape small balls from the dough, ensuring they are smooth and crack-free.
  4. Fry the balls in medium-hot oil/ghee until golden brown on all sides.
  5. Soak the fried balls in warm sugar syrup for 1–2 hours before serving.

Manchurian

Ingredients

  • Gobi
  • Cornfloar
  • Salt
  • Chilli Powder
  • Oil

Recipe

  1. Make batter by mixing maida, cornflour, salt, pepper, ginger-garlic paste, and water to form a thick coating.
  2. Dip cauliflower florets in the batter and deep-fry until golden and crispy; then set aside.
  3. Heat oil in a pan, sauté chopped garlic, ginger, green chilies, and spring onions for a minute.
  4. Add sauces like soy sauce, tomato ketchup, red chili sauce, vinegar, and a little water; cook till slightly thick.
  5. Toss fried gobi in the sauce, coat well, garnish with spring onions, and serve hot.

Biryani

Ingredients

  • Biryani Rice
  • 1kg Chiken
  • Oil
  • Onion
  • Spices
  • Salt
  • Curd

Recipe

  1. Marinate chicken with curd, ginger-garlic paste, red chili powder, turmeric, garam masala, salt, and lemon juice for 30 mins to 1 hour.
  2. Cook basmati rice till 70% done with whole spices (bay leaf, cloves, cardamom, cinnamon) and set aside.
  3. Fry onions till golden, then cook marinated chicken with onions, tomatoes, mint, and coriander until chicken is 80% done.
  4. Layer rice and chicken in a pot, sprinkle fried onions, mint, saffron milk, and ghee on top.
  5. Dum cook on low flame for 20-25 minutes (sealed with a lid) and serve hot with raita.

Paneer

Ingredients

  • Paneer
  • Chilli Powder
  • Oil
  • Onion
  • Spices
  • Salt
  • Ginger-Garlic Paste

Recipe

  1. Sauté onions, tomatoes, garlic, and ginger in butter, then blend into a smooth paste once cooled.
  2. Heat butter, add the paste, red chili powder, turmeric, garam masala, and cook till oil separates.
  3. Add cream and kasuri methi, mix well and simmer the gravy for a few minutes.
  4. Add paneer cubes and cook for 5-7 minutes on low flame until soft and coated.
  5. Garnish with cream and coriander, and serve hot with naan or rice.
